27 Sep 2024

The Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk has announced plans for renovations beginning next spring, focusing on repairing and modernizing existing attractions to enhance safety and visitor enjoyment. Key updates will include improvements to iconic rides, ensuring that safety measures are strengthened while preserving the nostalgic charm that has delighted generations. Alongside these upgrades, the Boardwalk aims to embrace sustainability through initiatives such as installing solar panels and offering eco-friendly practices.

Aesthetic enhancements will breathe new life into the Boardwalk, with vibrant signage, improved landscaping, and updated seating areas to create a more inviting atmosphere. Enhanced lighting will allow guests to enjoy the Boardwalk well into the evening, making it a year-round destination. As a cherished cultural landmark, the Boardwalk seeks community input on these renovations to ensure it continues to be a beloved destination for both locals and visitors.

Crime

Santa Cruz is facing a concerning 30% increase in property crimes, including burglaries and auto thefts, compared to the previous year. Local authorities attribute this spike to economic pressures stemming from the pandemic and reduced police staffing, which have hampered the department's ability to maintain a strong presence in the community.

In response to these rising crime rates, the Santa Cruz Police Department is launching community policing initiatives that promote collaboration between law enforcement and residents. These efforts include establishing neighborhood watch programs, increasing patrols in high-crime areas, and encouraging residents to adopt proactive measures to secure their homes and vehicles.
